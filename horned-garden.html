How to play

  • The garden hides horned creatures: Unicorns (1 horn), Bicorns (2), Tricorns (3) and Quadricorns (4). The counters in the palette show how many of each must be placed.
  • Creatures can never touch each other — not even diagonally.
  • The figures outside the garden count the creatures in that row or column. No figures means no creatures there.
  • A number inside the garden is the total of horns in the squares around it. Numbers and flowers are fixed — nothing can be placed on them.
  • Tap a square to place the selected creature; tap it again with the same type selected to remove it. Wrong placements are never blocked — the broken rule simply lights up red.
  • The hornless ? animal is a placeholder: an animal you have not identified yet. It counts as an animal on the board, but the garden only blooms once every ? has been given its horns.
  • Select the dark flower to mark squares that hold no creature. It is only a note: it does not affect the rules.
  • Every generated puzzle has exactly one solution. The moment the whole garden obeys every rule, it blooms.
  • Tap ✎ Create to design your own garden: place the number clues and flowers, and tap the outside squares to set the row & column counts — you don't place the animals, only the clues. Then hit ▶ Test to try solving your scheme. Authored gardens have no checks — anything goes.
